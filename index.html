About

I'm a graduating senior at UCSD double majoring in Cognitive Science (Language & Culture) and Interdisciplinary Computing & the Arts.

My obsession with memory didn't begin in a lab. It began with my grandfather — who fled North Korea during the Korean War, raised me with his hands and his stories, and is now slowly forgetting me through Alzheimer's. Living across Korea, France, and the United States, I kept asking the same question in different languages: what do we lose when we forget, and what stays in the body?

In France, I wrote philosophy essays about whether forgetting is necessary for happiness. Back in San Diego, I built an Arduino installation where a hand crank illuminates fading light — an attempt to make Alzheimer's felt, not just understood. In between, I analysed EEG signals, rendered tidal data across 20 screens, and photographed Korean objects as acts of remembrance.

I code, I research, and I make things that feel.
